0 Definitions[a]

This document contains the general terms and conditions for “Area FiftyLAN”, taking place from the 12th of June until the 14th of June 2015, from now on referenced as “the event”. The event is established by “Student Association for Mathematics and Computer Science Christiaan Huygens” through the “LANcie Committee 2014-2015” in cooperation with “Unit Sports and Culture”, these three parties from now on referenced as “the organization”. Any other individual legally present at, or has the authority to be present at, the event and is not part of the organization is considered a participant, if not stated otherwise. Every individual at the event must follow these terms and conditions.         

1 Validity[b]

The organization reserves the right of modifying these general terms and conditions until the start date of the event without any form of report of reason. The Dutch legislation is in effect at all times.

2 Tickets[c]

2.1 Purchasing tickets

Tickets can only be purchased through the official website: https://areafiftylan.nl. Tickets acquired through any other source will not be accepted. As will be mentioned when creating an order, purchasing a ticket automatically means the buyer accepts these general terms and conditions before you. Any data given will not be shared with third parties. Note that the official tickets will be digitally distributed two weeks prior to the event.

2.2 Refunds

Until three weeks before the event, meaning may 22nd 2015 is the last date, it is possible to refund any purchases made for the event. The relevant amount will be deposited on your bank account up to 2 weeks after the request for the refund has been made.

2.3 Reselling your ticket

Reselling a ticket and any services attached to it is only possible by notifying the organization through the official contact form on the website: https://areafiftylan.nl.

Make sure to set the subject to “Reselling Ticket” and to include in your message the following:

  • The current holder's full name
  • The full name of the new holder
  • The email address of the new holder

The reseller may charge the new buyer at maximum the original price; no profits must be made. The reseller is responsible for this exchange, including providing the organization with the right data.

2.4 Members of W.I.S.V. Christiaan Huygens

The event is, among others, hosted by the “Student Association for Mathematics and Computer Science Christiaan Huygens”. All members of this student association who wish to attend the event, can purchase a ticket for a lower price. Please note that your membership will be verified upon entering the event. If it appears you are not a CH member, but still purchased the corresponding ticket, you will be asked to pay the remaining costs for a regular ticket upon entrance.

2.5 Tickets are sold out

If tickets appear to be sold out, one can be put on a waiting list. There will be no second round for the ticket sale, meaning the waiting list will be the only way of getting a ticket. Once a ticket is available, you will be noted and will have 2 days after notification to purchase this ticket.

3 Participation[d]

3.1 Legal presence

Upon entering the event, every participant must be in possession of an official ticket and any of the following forms of identification: id-card, passport, drivers license. If the information on the ticket is not identical to the relevant information on the participants form of identification, he will be asked to leave the event. If the participant is younger than 18 years, any form of authorization by caretakers/ parents must be in possession as well.

Once the participant’s entry is approved, he will receive a bracelet he is obliged to wear during his stay at the event.

3.2 Illegal presence

During the event a participant’s proof of attendance is the bracelet given at arrival. If this bracelet is lost or damaged in an unrecognizable way, a new one must be obtained by rerunning the registration process. Without a bracelet, the organization will remove the person from the event.

3.3 Being[e] able to fully participate

Bringing a working PC with required accessories, including a network cable with a minimal length of 10 (ten) meters, is mandatory. The participant makes sure no illegal or harmful software is installed on his PC which can be spread through our network. This kind of software may not be brought in externally as well. The participant will make sure to have an up to date security system installed, e.g.  firewall and virusscanner, as the organization will not take responsibility for any damage.

3.4 Visitor

During the event, visiting without officially participating is possible and free of charge. Note that the organization can only allow a small group to enter the event at the same time. Upon entering, the visitor will be asked to accept the general terms and conditions and sign a form. Only by accepting will access be granted. The visitor will subsequently receive a bracelet  to be identified with. Without this bracelet,  presence at the event is not permitted. During his stay, the visitor will follow these general terms and conditions before you and is considered, where necessary, as a participant.

4 Liability[f]

Only the participant is responsible for his personal and intellectual properties. Any instructions given by the organization must be followed. In case of unacceptable behaviour, the participant may be given a warning or asked to leave immediately. A warning is given once, thereafter follows removal from the event. If a participant is asked to leave the event, no refunds will be given.

5 Illegal activities[g]

No weapons or other dangerous objects are allowed at the event. Hacking or spreading hazardous software, intentional or not, will result in removal from the event. If a participant is caught cheating in any way, he may not participate in any tournaments, including his current tournament. A participant will always coöperate with the organization when investigating sincere violations of the general terms and conditions. Otherwise, the participant will be removed from the event.

6 Behaviour[h] at the event

6.1 Undesired behaviour

The participant will not behave in a way that is unpleasant for his surroundings. Deliberately causing harm to another person or his property will not be tolerated and may result in removal from the event. In case of a fight, all responsible parties will be removed from the event, without negotiation. The participant will not cause noise disturbance, thus meaning speaker sets and other comparable objects are not allowed at the event.

6.2 Waste

Any garbage will be disposed of in the appropriate bins or bags. Do not litter or leave unwanted objects behind after the event.

6.3 Promoting during the event

6.3.1 Advertising

No advertising is allowed without the organization’s permission. If the participant has a sponsor or other party which would like to use the event as a promotion site, please contact the organization beforehand. If unauthorized advertising is discovered, the person responsible will be asked to leave the event and of €250,- will be levied.

6.3.2 Sales

Sales, which are not commissioned by the organization, are not allowed at the event. If a participant is found selling products in any form, he will be removed from the event and a fine of  €250,- will be levied.  

6.3.3 Surveys

Surveying at the event is allowed after the permission is granted by the organization. If unauthorized surveying is detected, the person responsible will be removed from the event. In case this person is a participant, he will be given a warning. If the participant is found to continue his activities after a warning was given, he will be removed from the event as well.

6.4 Alcohol[i]/ narcotics

Alcohol consumption is only permitted above the age of 18. Drunkenness will not be tolerated, with the risk of being removed from the event. Soft- and hard drugs, medication excluded, are not permitted at the event. Smoking is only permitted outside.

7 Food[j] and beverages

7.1 Bringing food and drinks to the event

During the event, breakfast, lunch and a BBQ will be provided. In addition to that, participants may bring limited food and drinks of their own. Food delivery to the address of the event is not allowed.

7.2 Amenities[k] regarding food and beverages

During the official time of period of the event, the organization will provide the participants with up to three meals a day. These meals are included in the price of the ticket. Please refer to the official timetable for more information.

Outside these meals participants may turn to their own limited supply, free snacks and beverages provided by the organization or purchase meals and drinks at the Sports Cafe.

Consumption is only permitted in the main hall or outside.

9 Media[l]

Video recordings and photographs will be made at the event . No compensation can be demanded for this. The organization is not responsible for agreements with third parties (e.g. publication of photographs). Personal video- and audio recordings may not be used for commercial purposes.

10 Tournaments

10.1 Registration

It is allowed to participate in multiple tournaments as long as the tournaments do not overlap. As soon as a competitions will overlap, the participant will need to pick a tournament to continue with and drop the conflicting tournament. Only complete teams can start with the tournament, it is also required to have a team captain whom will be the central point of communication between the team and the organisation.

10.2 Prizes[m]

The organization reserves the right to modify the prizes according to the number of registrations or other circumstances. The prizewinner(s) shall be presented at the ceremony and will be personally awarded his prize, unless a substitute is made known to the organization.

10.3 Game Masters

For the requirements and to submit your plan, please refer to our website. We will review your proposal, and when accepted, we’ll appoint you as responsible person to run this tournament. This means the entire organisation of the tournament will be up to you. Your tournament will be placed on the website, but we can not help you with the practical implementation of your tournament.

11 Accommodations[n]

During the event overnight stay is recommended.There is a separate hall available for sleeping where you can set up your air mattress or any other direct form of sleeping equipment. Structures as tents are not allowed. It is also not allowed to sleep behind or around your computer.

12 Prohibited objects

Any object, not directly needed for gaming and needs electricity to function is not permitted at the event. Exceptions are chargers for gadgets like mobile phones. Below is a list of objects which are not allowed at the event due to unnecessary, safety reasons and rules. Note that any similar objects that are not listed and have the same effect or function are also prohibited at the event.

  • coolboxes
  • refrigerators
  • microwaves
  • fan/ air conditioners
  • tents
  • speaker(sets)

13 Services

13. 1 Pickup[o] Service

The participant can sign up for the non-free pickup service upon registration. This service is only available for participants living in a radius of 7.5 (seven and a half) kilometers around the event location. All PC’s will be collected on thursday June 11th.  The participant will be noted beforehand of the exact moment this pickup will occur.

For this service a few requirements must be met and if they are not, the collector may refuse to take your PC. These requirements are listed below.

  1. This service is for PC’s and its necessary accessories only, meaning other objects will be rejected.
  2. The PC and accessories must be fully and neatly packed in stackable boxes.
  3. Every box must consist of a label containing:
  1. The participants full name and address
  2. The total number of boxes and the current box’s number

Note that the use of this service is under full responsibility of the participant. The organization will treat your possessions with the utmost care, but is not liable for any inconveniences.

At the event the participant can collect his PC and accessories if he holds a proper form of id (see section 3.1) which's information corresponds with the information on the boxes.

Nevertheless, it is illegal to reproduce any copyrighted material, without the author’s permission. Any possible illegal movement of protected software between participants, is the participant’s responsibility. The organization will not accept any liability for violating privacy. The organization ensures the participant’s privacy as far as she is legally required. Hence, every participant is responsible for using any sort of software. Therefore, the organization will not accept liability for content, use, ownership, reliability or accuracy. For software provided by the event these rules do not apply, in this case the event will take full responsibility.
