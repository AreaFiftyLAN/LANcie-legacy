Area FiftyLAN

Area FiftyLAN is the hottest new gaming event of the TU Delft. This spectacular all-weekend event, from 12 till 14 June 2015, will be organized in cooperation with Unit Sports & Culture. All students are welcome bring their PC's or laptops to play their favorite games with their fellow students and friends. You decide what to play! There will be exciting competitive tournaments like FIFA, League of Legends and Trackmania. If you're not into competitive gaming however, no problem! You can play all the games you want, whether that's The Sims, Age of Empires 2, Rollercoaster Tycoon or Minecraft, you decide!

That is not all! There will be plenty to do offline as well. Poker and foosball masters can also show off in this event. In the Launch Area, you'll find pinball machines, airhockey tables, table football tables and various retro gaming consoles. An overview of things you can do besides gaming can be found below.

What do you get?

A base ticket will be €35*. This includes admission to the entire event, breakfast and lunch on saturday and sunday, and a BBQ on saturday. You will have your own desk of 1,10m x 0,5m for the weekend, and a comfy chair to sit on. For an additional €2,50, we will pick up your PC thursday evening and deliver it to the event. We will return it to you sunday evening. Area FiftyLAN will be an unforgettable event you do not want to miss! Are you ready for the launch?
*CH members/alumni will get a small discount

Organize your own tournament!

Is your favorite game not listed as a tournament? No problem! We offer you the possibility to become a game master! For this, we would like to receive a proposal for your tournament before May 15th. Your proposal should contain the following information

  1. Which game you want to host
  2. Game format (Free-for-all, 5vs5, 1vs1, etc)
  3. Tournament structure (Qualifiers, rounds and a final, or a single round)
  4. Winner criteria (How is the winner chosen?)
  5. Expected duration (4 hours for the entire tournament, or does it span multiple days?)
  6. Required resources (Do you need our server, or host your own?)
  7. Contact information

Official tournaments have priority, which means your tournament should be flexible enough be held at any point during the weekend. We prefer short compact tournaments, but don't let that stop you from proposing an amazing multi-day event. There is no limit to the duration of the tournament, but the format should be taken into account. A truly competitive tournament should not take more than a morning or afternoon, while a casual long term tournaments can span multiple days.

We will review your proposal, and when accepted, we’ll appoint you as responsible person to run this tournament. This means the entire organisation of the tournament will be up to you. Your tournament will be placed on the website. but we can not offer any extra support for your tournament during the event.
